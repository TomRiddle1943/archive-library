# Horcrux Theory

> Research Log
> Author: T.M. Riddle
> Classification: Private

## Definition

A Horcrux is an object intentionally bound to a fragment of one's own soul.

The act is considered among the darkest forms of magic ever documented, not because of the enchantment itself, but because of the sacrifice required to create one.

Reliable sources remain exceptionally scarce.

---

## Objectives

- Determine whether multiple Horcruxes are theoretically possible.
- Verify the permanence of soul fragmentation.
- Compare conflicting historical accounts.
- Identify rituals omitted from publicly accessible texts.

---

## Current Findings

The Hogwarts library contains numerous references to soul magic, yet almost all descriptions terminate before practical implementation.

Entire sections appear deliberately omitted or redacted.

Whether this is due to censorship or loss of knowledge remains uncertain.

Professor Slughorn may possess additional insight.

---

## Working Notes

Questions requiring further investigation:

- Does repeated fragmentation reduce magical ability?
- Is there a practical limit to the number of fragments?
- Can a damaged soul maintain stability indefinitely?
- Are certain objects more suitable than others?
- Can a Horcrux be detected without prior knowledge?

---

## Observation

Knowledge this valuable is never destroyed.

It is merely hidden.

If one source refuses to provide answers, another must be found.

Every incomplete record suggests the existence of a more complete one.
